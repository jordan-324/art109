Missed Connections (2025)

Installation

An interactive art installation made in collaboration by Jordan Medina and Jared Gacusan in conjunction with the San Jose Museum of Art's Art Learning Lab Program. A two part piece installation of a single-channel video of a live security camera that displays viewers through a pepper's ghost illusion as they approach the piece.

"How do memories live on without a way of recording them? A monolithic surveillance mirror displays the engagement of visitors by using a security camera and a pepper's ghost illusion to make them appear as if a hologram. Visitors are asked to engage and interact with the work in these spaces. The work depends on the interaction of a viewer for completion. As the viewer observes the piece, the piece simultaneously observes the viewer. After that, the piece temporarily displays its observations of the viewer, as a performance part of the art. Forcing a looping state of introspection between art and viewer — until one component of the loop leaves the presence of the other. If your image disappears as you step out of the piece's presence: Where does it go?"
