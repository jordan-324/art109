Momento (2025)

Single-Channel Videp

I chose objects that are typically used to track and trace time. My goal was to put an emphasis on these items and to bring out different feelings through their presentation. I found that many of these objects bring back different memories to me, as they all ended up in my art studio at different times, and with different stories. All of these items represent a passage in time. For example, Every time a candle is used, its appearance is altered. In the past, people would use this to track how much time had passed since it was lit. In my video, I think of all the candles shot as a human life and a sort of story appears. The clock symbolizes a pause in time. The flashing "12:00 am" always brings me memories from when I was a child and the clocks in my kitchen needed to be reset. It was probably the first time in my entire life I had ever thought to myself "what if time stayed still?" The camera represents capturing a visualization of time and space. I think the camera is one of the greatest creations of mankind. Aside from making art with it, it is a way for humans to actually see a glimpse into their past. The CD, similar to how the camera documents and records memory, is a place for audio to be stored. Hearing certain songs or sounds can trigger associative memories that people may have forgotten about. For example, when a song you haven't heard in a while comes on and you think to yourself, "This brings me back to middle school!" The mouse toy is a personal memento of mine, I wanted to also express physical forms of memory. For example, oftentimes people will collect trinkets and such when on vacation, as a reminder of the time that they spent there. I shot all of the objects I had with an old Sony Handycam rigged up to an old CRT tv, I then shot the screen so that I had captured the natural grain from the old tv. I became very focused on capturing this grain, and playing with light and reflections because I felt they expressed a message of time in the present moment. I used many practical effects for my project through set designing, but I also used a ton of different effects on the video. Such as Various blurs, dissolves, lighting, time remapping, masks, lumetri, blending options, 3D and more. I lost track of everything I used, however I used most effects in a way to make the video feel somewhat seamless, but also having different "sections" throughout. In the end sequence I wanted to break free of any structure. A liminal space, full of boundless memories. I created this section to feel like life flashing before one's eyes.
