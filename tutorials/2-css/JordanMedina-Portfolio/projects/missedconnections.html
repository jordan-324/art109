Missed Connections (2025)

Airbrush on Canvas

Missed Connections explores the awkward translation of human connection through everyday life and digital platforms. The work was inspired by scrolling through Craigslist’s “Missed Connections” section and considering the small, missed moments that lead people to attempt reconnection through technology.

Attraction in public spaces often goes unspoken due to timing, hesitation, or social restraint. Technology extends that moment, offering a second chance at connection—even if it is uncertain or unlikely.

In this painting, a scene unfolds on a subway where two strangers share a brief moment of mutual recognition. One later posts to “Missed Connections,” describing small details—matcha, a plastic bag, a glance—hoping the other person might respond. The work leaves the outcome unresolved, questioning whether the imagined reunion is real, remembered, or purely constructed desire.
