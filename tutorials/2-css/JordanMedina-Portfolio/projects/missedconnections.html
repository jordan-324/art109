Missed Connections (2025)

Airbrush on Canvas

I wanted to channel the awkward translation of human connection through human life and technology. I got the idea by scrolling through Craigslist’s “Missed Connections” tab and thought about the instances that must take place to actually use this feature. For example, attraction in everyday life can take place, However, because of antisocial behaviors you may not be able to act on this attraction and make a connection with this person in this time frame. This is where technology gives you an extended time frame to making this connection happen, even though it can be a complete shot in the dark. In the example of my painting I set a scene where attraction takes place on a subway, the person remembers the other person by small details like the drink and color of their plastic bag. They then write “We sat face to face on the morning train southbound. Unable to stop our eyes from meeting. You sipped matcha and listened to music I couldn’t hear. Your stop arrived before my courage could. Words tethered to my throat, breath held tight within my chest. The train pulled away and so did my chance. By the grace of fate, if you see this and felt the fleeting moment too. Reply with the color of your bag — so I’ll know it’s you. I’d love another chance.” to the Missed Connections tab in hopes of making a connection. I then leave the situation open to interpretation where a heart in the middle plays with a scene of the two kissing, making the viewer ask themselves if the scene is something that takes place in the future or a fantasy of desperation.
