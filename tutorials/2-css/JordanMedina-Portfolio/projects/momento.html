Momento (2025)

Single-Channel Video

Momento focuses on objects that function as markers of time, memory, and personal history. Each object in the video represents a different relationship to duration and experience.

Candles, clocks, cameras, CDs, and personal trinkets are presented as carriers of memory. Each one contains a different way humans attempt to measure, store, or recall time.

The candle reflects gradual transformation through use, the clock interrupts time with static repetition, and the camera becomes a tool for preserving lived experience. The CD stores sound as memory, while small personal objects act as physical anchors to specific moments in life.

The video was shot using a Sony Handycam and displayed through a CRT monitor to emphasize analog distortion and visual grain. Additional effects—such as time remapping, blur, and layering—were used to shift between structured segments and unstable memory states.

The final sequence dissolves structure entirely, forming a liminal space where memory, time, and perception collapse into a continuous flow.
