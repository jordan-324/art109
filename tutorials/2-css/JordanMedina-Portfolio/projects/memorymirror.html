memorymirror (2023)

Installation

An interactive installation made in collaboration between Jordan Medina and Jared Gacusan in conjunction with the San Jose Museum of Art’s Art Learning Lab program.

The work consists of a single-channel live security camera feed combined with a Pepper’s Ghost illusion, allowing viewers to see themselves as a projected presence as they approach the piece.

“How do memories live on without a way of recording them? A monolithic surveillance mirror displays the engagement of visitors using a live camera system and optical illusion. Visitors become both observer and observed. As the viewer interacts with the piece, the work simultaneously records and reflects them, creating a loop of mutual surveillance and introspection. When the viewer leaves, the image disappears—raising the question: where does it go?”
